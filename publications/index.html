  1. FoV-Net: Rotation-Invariant CAD B-rep Learning via Field-of-View Ray Casting
    Matteo Ballegeer and Dries F. Benoit
    In IEEE/CVF International Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition, Jun 2026
    Abs arXiv Bib

    Learning directly from boundary representations (B-reps) has significantly advanced 3D CAD analysis. However, state-of-the-art B-rep learning methods rely on absolute coordinates and normals to encode global context, making them highly sensitive to rotations. We introduce FoV-Net, the first B-rep learning framework that captures both local surface geometry and global structural context in a rotation-invariant manner using field-of-view ray casting.

  2. BenDFM: A taxonomy and synthetic CAD dataset for manufacturability assessment in sheet metal bending
    Matteo Ballegeer and Dries F. Benoit
    Journal of Intelligent Manufacturing, 2026
    Accepted
    Abs arXiv Bib

    Predicting the manufacturability of CAD designs early is crucial for Design for Manufacturing. We propose a taxonomy of manufacturability metrics and introduce BenDFM, the first synthetic dataset for manufacturability assessment in sheet metal bending with 20,000 parts, both manufacturable and unmanufacturable, generated with process-aware bending simulations.

  1. Evaluating the stability of model explanations in instance-dependent cost-sensitive credit scoring
    Matteo Ballegeer, Matthias Bogaert, and Dries F. Benoit
    European Journal of Operational Research, Nov 2025
    Abs DOI Bib

    We investigate the stability of instance-dependent cost-sensitive (IDCS) classifiers and their SHAP/LIME explanations under class imbalance. Our results show that explanations applied to such models are significantly less stable, especially under high class imbalance, with implications for banking regulations on transparency and explainability.
